# Anime Source Guide

Use this guide when recommending, scoring, or summarizing reception.

## Primary Chinese/Japanese Signals

- Bangumi: best single Chinese-language score/watchlist/discussion source for anime fans. Use score, votes, rank, comments, discussion board, and followers separately.
- Bahamut Anime: useful for Taiwan/HK/Macau streaming availability, episode discussion, and audience heat.
- Bilibili: useful for mainland Chinese discussion, clips, memes, comments, and topic heat when available.
- Douban: useful for anime films, older titles, mainstream audience sentiment, and out-of-fandom reception.
- Japanese X: useful for real-time buzz, memes, controversy, and official hashtag activity.
- Niconico/ABEMA: useful for Japanese live reaction and streaming heat.
- Filmarks: Japanese general-audience scores and reviews for movies, dramas, and anime.
- Anikore: Japanese anime-user scoring and reviews; check sample size and possible volatility.

## Secondary International Signals

- MAL and AniList: useful for international popularity and broad score comparison.
- Reddit/r-anime: useful for English-community episode reactions.
- IMDb: useful mainly for globally known series and films.

## Evaluation Method

1. Verify current date and episode count for airing anime.
2. Map title aliases across Chinese, Japanese, and English.
3. Check score and sample size where available.
4. Check discussion heat separately from score.
5. Summarize why communities praise or criticize the show.
6. Recommend by fit category rather than pure ranking.

## Early-Season Caution

For shows with only 1-3 episodes aired, treat scores as provisional. Favor first-impression wording such as "目前反馈", "首集口碑", and "建议先试一集".
